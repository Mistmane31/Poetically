"the moon is shy no more"

by s.a.poetics


My sole dearest, the moon is shy no more.
For it returns to whom it’s shining for.
Though glow is borrowed, ’tis I who chooses
To beam, at the eyes where stars fill spaces.

Come and bask in your softest throne—the Earth.
Where Night sets all in motion as the birthed
Epitome of no surpassing sway
under the light that fades in sight of day.

O, Light invisible, reach a firm stay!
All too short: The Night over yesterday.
But ’tis I who take Sun’s identity.
Failure to have light in Independency.

— April 10, 2024

Themes

The poem talks about the feelings of regret, frustration, and anxiety. Regret for the speaker cannot have their own courage to freely love and embrace their muse; the one they deem to be beyond "surpassing," meaning perfect. Frustration at the short time that a borrowed courage from someone else lasts until they are no longer fearless to attempt to love. And anxiety towards their muse, for they may not want an inconsistent display of affection from someone, so long as the speaker cannot find an infinite source of "light," they continue to deny yet worry for the future.
