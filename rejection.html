"Rejection"

by s.a.poetics


Night is not for noise to hoist;
Ill-advised as for today.
The crowd’s cheer has lost its voice
to such distressing display.

As the man who was trembling,
before his heavy thoughts freed.
His eyes felt their eyes leaning
beyond what his clenches breed.

Ungently, did the other land
unsweet than he expected;
Towards the paper in hand,
Phrases retaliated.

Only a word upon his;
His world, tender if of two.
He would know no other bliss,
and let that world continue.

But a murmur of Time's sense:
Pain is brief as one's Steppings.
Though Courage and Confidence
appear in discerned musings,
can be in one existence;
Acceptance of Impendings.

— June 23, 2024

Themes

The poem revolves around the idea of facing and acknowledging the fate of rejection from someone we admire. Beset on a confession, the poem narrates the bittersweet moment that the one who confessed hears the exact opposite of their expectation. While the latter part of the poem directly addresses the reader and attempts to ease the confessor, that looking into how their confidence allowed them to speak their truth, their courage to let the feeling go altogether after rejection is truly the path where someone finds their means to be free and wiser.
