"A Little Semblance"

by s.a.poetics


O, the dusk, when the sun’s gleam fades,
When the moon will no longer meet us near,
One memory of you erodes in shades
dark as night, yet not quite ceasing my rear.

Minute, my reply to the warmest greet
of my lover's first morning—daily do's.
For skin hesitates to this kind of heat
—foreign to me—as if a fault of you.

Subtle details I could not say I sought;
Now as dawn does it tend my quiet needs through.
But even with his ardent words, my heart
still desires a little semblance of you.

— October 4, 2024

Themes

The poem interchangeably jumps the past and the present. Where the speaker romanticizes the present in a somber way, for there remnants that still remind them of a past relationship. That the present gives a breather for the speaker because of their new relationship, albeit unlike the intensity they share from their past partner. Highlighting the dilemma between longing and acceptance, the speaker blurs the times with the old and the new.
