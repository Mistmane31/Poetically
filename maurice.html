"Maurice"

by s.a.poetics


Until you see, only the lonely stars shine,
and feel Regret hug you tight to the chest;
And see no other glass can hold your wine,
And feel no admiration when deep at rest.

There is a voice in you that speaks for me.
Like a parrot, frequent to reutter—
"Even everydays are evergreen;
For our hands like petals, guards the Flower"

But alas, half the petals had forsook
the budding Flower, warm and innocent.
And the petals’ reply—in time did it took—
afraid, for Flower grows more unsilent.

— October 13, 2024

Themes

The poem's title references the 1987 movie: Maurice. Which is about a sad queer love story with a bittersweet ending.

While the poem's content revolves around a warning, that a great love such as theirs could haunt the two of them should they be apart while it's yet to bloom. Metaphorizing Love as of a bud, that petals are them protecting it and nurturing it until it's ready to see the outside world.

However, the poem also involves the reality of being in a relationship of the same-sex. The expectations, prejudice, judgements, and monikers society will thrust upon two people can be pressuring. That some couples may not succeed to see it through, for a certain mindset of avoiding a problem by stopping to be the cause can be much safer than risking it.
